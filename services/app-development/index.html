Frequently Asked

Questions, answered.

How much does it cost to build a custom app? +

It depends on scope, but most small-to-mid-business apps land in the $50K to $120K range industry-wide, and a lean MVP costs a fraction of a full build. We scope to the smallest version that delivers real value, price it transparently after your evaluation, and never pad it with features you don't need yet.

How long does it take to develop an app? +

A realistic MVP is 8 to 16 weeks, not a year. The thing that blows up timelines is scope creep, so we fix scope up front and ship in visible sprints. You'll have running software in your hands early, not a big reveal at the end.

Do you build for both iOS and Android? +

Yes — plus web, from a single codebase. That means one build instead of three, and fixes and features that land on every platform at once instead of costing you triple.

Do I own the code and the intellectual property? +

Completely, in writing — the code, repository, designs, and accounts, yours from day one. You're never renting your product and never locked to us to keep it running.

Do you handle App Store and Google Play submission? +

Yes. Store submission, review guidelines, rejections, and resubmits are our job. We take it through to live and downloadable — launch is part of the deliverable, not something we hand back to you.

Can we start with an MVP instead of the full build? +

That's usually the smart move. We build a real MVP with a real backend and real data, get it in front of users, and let their behavior guide what we build next. You don't need to raise money first to start; you need a clear first version.

Who maintains and updates the app after launch? +

We offer ongoing support and iteration — OS updates, store policy changes, bug fixes, and new features. And because you own everything, you're free to keep it in-house or take it elsewhere. The choice stays yours.

Why custom instead of no-code like Bubble or FlutterFlow? +

No-code is great for a quick prototype, and we'll say so when it fits. But teams hit real walls: performance issues at scale, no code export so you're locked to the platform, and rising subscription costs. If you're building something you intend to grow and own, custom is what actually scales — and it's genuinely yours.
