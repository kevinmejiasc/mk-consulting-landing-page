Frequently Asked

Questions, answered.

How fast can an engineer start? +

Usually days, not months. After a short scoping call we can typically have a senior engineer working on your build within about a week — compared to the two to three months a full-time hire takes just to fill, plus more to ramp up.

Is this part-time or full-time? +

Either. You set the dial: a fractional engineer a few days a week, a full-time embedded engineer, or a squad for a project. Scale it up for a launch and back down after.

How senior are the engineers, and are they US-based? +

Senior and US-based, embedded in your team and timezone. The work is founder-led — Kevin is hands-on across every engagement — so you get judgment and ownership, not junior hours you have to manage closely.

Do I own the code and IP? +

Yes, entirely. Everything is committed to your repositories under your accounts as we build, and IP transfers to you in writing. If we ever part ways, you keep all of it — no lock-in, no hostage code.

Can I scale the team up or down? +

That's the model. Add capacity for a sprint, pull it back between phases, pause and resume. You pay for what the work needs this month, not a permanent headcount you're stuck with next year.

How does pricing work — hourly, retainer, or per project? +

Flexible to the engagement. Ongoing embedded work is a monthly retainer; defined projects and backlog sprints are scoped by deliverable. You get a clear estimate after the free consult, with no mystery pricing.

How is this different from just hiring? +

Hiring is right for a permanent core role you'll grow for years — it takes two to three months and a six-figure first-year cost, and carries real risk if it's the wrong person. On-demand engineering skips the search and the fixed cost: you get shipping capacity now, flexibly, and can decide on a full-time hire later from a position of work already done.

What tech stacks do you work in, and how do you use AI? +

Modern web and mobile stacks, plus the integration and automation layer around them. We build with current AI tooling as part of delivery, which lets a small senior team ship at a pace that used to require a much larger one — and we can build AI features into the product itself when it helps.
